Interpretation

The discussion moves beyond description and explains how the results answer the research question. It connects empirical observations with the theoretical framework developed earlier.

A persuasive interpretation is careful about scope. It shows what the findings support, what they complicate, and where alternative explanations may still be plausible.

Theoretical Implications

This section explains how the findings relate to the theoretical background. It can show whether the results support, refine, or challenge the concepts introduced earlier.

Methodological Reflections

This part reflects on how the chosen method shaped the findings. It can address the strengths of the research design and note where another method may have produced a different perspective.

Practical Implications

This section explains why the findings matter beyond the immediate analysis. The implications can be practical, institutional, policy-oriented, or relevant for future academic work.

  • How the findings relate to the literature
  • What they suggest about the selected case or phenomenon
  • Which questions remain open for future research

Limitations

Limitations strengthen the paper when they are addressed transparently. They define the boundaries of the argument and help the reader assess the reliability of the conclusions.

A limitation is not a weakness by itself; it is a boundary that makes the claim more precise.

Future Research

Future research can build on the paper by expanding the material, applying the framework to other cases, or examining questions that remained outside the paper's scope.
