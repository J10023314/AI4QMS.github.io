Chapter 01

Theoretical Background

The chapter establishes the concepts, models, and scholarly debates around AI, quality management, and accounting firm governance.

On This Page

Key Concepts Literature Context Analytical Framework

Key Concepts

The theoretical background begins by clarifying the central concepts used throughout the paper. Each concept should be defined precisely and connected to the research question so that the later analysis has a stable foundation.

Definitions should not stand alone. They become useful when they reveal relationships: causes and effects, actors and institutions, tensions between different explanations, or the conditions under which a concept becomes relevant.

Literature Context

This section situates the paper within existing scholarship. It identifies the most important authors, perspectives, and debates, while also showing where the paper's own focus enters the conversation.

The theoretical chapter should make the reader understand not only what the paper studies, but also why this way of studying it is convincing.

A strong literature context compares positions instead of listing them. It can show agreement, disagreement, missing evidence, or unresolved questions that motivate the analysis.

Analytical Framework

The final part of the chapter turns the literature review into a working framework. This framework defines the lens through which the empirical material or case will be examined.

  • Concepts that guide the analysis
  • Assumptions derived from the literature
  • Criteria used to interpret the findings
