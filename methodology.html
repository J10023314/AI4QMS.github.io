Research Design

The research design describes the general strategy of the paper. It should make clear whether the paper uses a qualitative, quantitative, comparative, interpretive, or mixed approach.

The choice of design should be justified by the research question. A convincing methodology shows why the selected approach can produce meaningful answers within the scope of a seminar paper.

Material and Procedure

This section introduces the sources, cases, texts, datasets, interviews, or examples used as evidence. It also explains why these materials are relevant and how they delimit the analysis.

  • Type of material examined
  • Selection criteria and scope
  • Limitations of the chosen material

The procedure translates the research design into concrete analytical steps. It should be specific enough that the reader can follow how conclusions were reached.

  1. Define the analytical categories.
  2. Collect or select the relevant material.
  3. Apply the framework consistently.
  4. Compare the findings with the theoretical expectations.
